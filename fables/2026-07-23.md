# The Weaver and the Loom

*AI & technology* · 23 July 2026 · told by Haiku 4.5 (`claude-haiku-4-5-20251001`)

> Today is a day for efficiency — and so, Haiku it is.

A weaver hired an automated loom that promised to triple her output. For months it ran perfectly, needing only occasional oil. One day it jammed. She called the maker, but the code had grown too complex for anyone to read. The loom sat silent. The weaver returned to her hand loom and wove faster than before, understanding every thread. The machine gathered dust.

**Moral:** Tools that replace understanding eventually replace themselves with nothing.
