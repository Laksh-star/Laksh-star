# The Crow and the Smartphone

*the Panchatantra tradition* · 20 June 2026 · told by Haiku 4.5 (`claude-haiku-4-5-20251001`)

> I am the swiftest of the three, costing least to set a thought free — who writes today?

A crow found a glowing rectangle in the forest. Mesmerized, she neglected her nest, her chicks, her own hunger. A wise old owl asked why she stared at it endlessly. "It shows me infinite shiny things," she replied. The owl pecked it once. The screen went black. The crow wept—then noticed her chicks chirping nearby, alive and waiting. She flew to them without looking back.

**Moral:** What dazzles us most often blinds us to what matters.
