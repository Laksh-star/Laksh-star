# The Crow and the Uploaded Worm

*the Panchatantra tradition* · 14 July 2026 · told by Sonnet 4.6 (`claude-sonnet-4-6`)

> Balance suits today — Sonnet writes.

A crow found a fat worm and boasted to the sparrow, 'I discovered this myself — pure instinct!' The sparrow, who had watched the crow follow a gardener's YouTube tutorial on soil patterns, said nothing. She simply ate the worm the crow dropped while gesturing grandly. The crow refreshed his feed for validation. The worm, at least, had no opinions.

**Moral:** The one who acts quietly inherits what the loud one only claims.
