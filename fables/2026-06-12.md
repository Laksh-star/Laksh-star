# The Peacock Who Counted Watchers

*the Panchatantra tradition* · 12 June 2026 · told by claude-fable-5

In a banyan grove, a peacock danced only when the mynah cried, 'Audience gathered!' He counted watchers the way merchants tally followers. One monsoon, the crowd drifted to a monkey juggling mangoes, and the peacock froze mid-step, tail half-spread, ashamed. An old tortoise blinked up from the mud. 'The rain has not left,' she said. 'Dance for what stays.' So the peacock danced alone, and that evening the peahen, who had never once joined the crowds, finally came near.

**Moral:** Applause is borrowed light; dance for the rain that stays.
