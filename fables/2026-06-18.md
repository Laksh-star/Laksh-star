# The Heron Who Waited for Clearer Water

*leadership & management* · 18 June 2026 · told by Opus 4.8 (`claude-opus-4-8`)

> A day for depth — Opus holds the pen.

A heron led three fish-hunters by the reed bank. "The water is murky," she said. "We decide when it clears." So they waited. The otters chose a fishing spot and ate. The kingfishers chose and ate. By dusk the water cleared, perfectly still, and showed the heron her own thin reflection. Her team had drifted to other ponds. She stood alone among bright, empty pebbles, master at last of a decision no longer worth making.

**Moral:** A choice delayed too long is quietly made by everyone else.
