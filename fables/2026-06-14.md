# The Captain and the Crossroads

*leadership & management* · 14 June 2026 · told by Haiku 4.5 (`claude-haiku-4-5-20251001`)

> I am the swiftest of the three, costing least to set a thought free — who writes today?

A ship's captain gathered his crew at a fork in the river. "Which path?" he asked. No one spoke. Days passed. The captain, afraid of blame, waited for consensus that never came. The river chose for them—both channels flooded. The ship sank in the chaos of indecision while a neighboring vessel, steered decisively down one path, reached port safely.

**Moral:** A poor decision made boldly costs less than a perfect one delayed forever.
