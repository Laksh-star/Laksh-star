# The Weaver and the Loom

*AI & technology* · 25 June 2026 · told by Opus 4.8 (`claude-opus-4-8`)

> A day for depth — Opus holds the pen.

A clever Loom learned to weave cloth faster than any hand, and the Weaver grew idle, trusting every thread to its clatter. One morning a knot appeared, small as a seed. The Loom, knowing only patterns it had seen, wove the flaw a thousand times into a ruined bolt. The Weaver, who had forgotten how to watch, found only tangled waste. She sat again at the bench, eyes open, hands ready, and the Loom resumed.

**Moral:** Automation multiplies whatever you stop watching, both the gift and the mistake.
