# The Peacock and the Sparrow

*the Panchatantra tradition* · 06 July 2026 · told by Haiku 4.5 (`claude-haiku-4-5-20251001`)

> Today is a day for efficiency — and so, Haiku it is.

A peacock mocked a sparrow for her drab feathers and small size. 'I am admired by thousands,' he boasted, fanning his brilliant tail. The sparrow said nothing. Years passed. The peacock's feathers faded and fell. His joints ached; he could barely walk. The sparrow, meanwhile, flew freely across mountains and seas, her small frame tireless. One dawn, the peacock watched her disappear into the horizon and wept.

**Moral:** Beauty fades; usefulness endures across seasons.
